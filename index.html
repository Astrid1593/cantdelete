Message from the Principal

Welcome to Fleetwood Primary School, a school community where students are safe, happy and learning. At our school, programs are designed to meet your child's individual learning needs. Our school motto 'Achieving Excellence Together' is an indication of our commitment to work with families to ensure each child achieves success. We aim to assist your child to achieve both socially and academically through their primary years. We believe education is every child's right and as a school we have the responsibility to ensure that each child reaches their full potential. Our 'Home/School Partnership' is an effective way in which we support student learning. Each family signs a 'Partnership Agreement' designed to assist communication between home and school and to support student learning. Parents are encouraged to take an active interest in their child's education by becoming involved in classroom support programs, attending community events and celebrations, joining School Council or our Fleetwood And Community Events committee (F.A.C.E). Should any concerns arise regarding your child's educational progress over their primary school years, your first point of contact is your child's teacher. On behalf of the staff and the School Council, I look forward to sharing an exciting time with you and your child as they learn, develop and achieve their best at Fleetwood Primary School.

Tobin Cuss, Acting Principal

Interschool Sport Events

District Cross Country

This week on May 7th, all the students that were in top 9 of their Cross Country races went into District, where students would race against other schools. The event took place at Maranatha Christian School. Some students' parents came to cheer them on. The age groups were 9-10, 11yo and 12-13. The top 10 students who ran in each race would go onto divisionals. Out of the (I think) 30-40 students, three got into into Divisionals, the next stage of Cross Country.



District Swimming

Students took part in racing other schools to swim 50m of any two swimming strokes they wanted to. Tim Bowden (PE teacher) was a great help.
